Repositories:

Git repositories are a collection of files, of several different versions, of a project. They are typically synchronized with some online hosting service such as github to allow a safer backup or collaboration.


Commits:

Commits are snapshots of a repository at a specific point in time, typically associated with a message and often synchronized with a remote hosting service at this point.


Branches:

Git branches are independent lines of development from each other, often a program is branched from master into a separate branch that is developed in to add a new feature while keeping a stable version of the project.


Tags:

Tags are used to capture a specific point in a program's history, they are distinct from branches as they do not have further history of commits after creation. They are generally used to point to version releases.


Merges:

Merging is an action a user can take to reconcile separate development histories that are contained in branches. One branches changes are enveloped into another branch, for example feat1 into master.
